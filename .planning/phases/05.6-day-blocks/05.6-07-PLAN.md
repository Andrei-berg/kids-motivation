<objective>
Close CR-02 (code half): replace the client-rotatable idempotency key on the
`custom_block` award with a deterministic key derived from the natural
`(child_id, date, block_id)` tuple, and close WR-01 by family-scoping the
`day_block_entries` read in the award route.

Today `app/api/wallet/award/route.ts:569` uses `sourceId: entry.id` — the
`day_block_entries` row's own UUID. Because entries RLS grants family-wide
INSERT/UPDATE/DELETE, a child can delete + re-insert the entry to rotate the UUID
and defeat the `(child_id, source_type, source_id)` unique index, minting
unbounded coins. `child_id` is already part of the idempotency tuple, so keying
on `` `${date}:${entry.block_id}` `` makes the key stable regardless of how many
times the entry row is rotated. WR-01: the entries query filters only
`child_id`+`date`; adding `family_id` prevents the service-role read from picking
up a cross-family row inserted for the same child id.

Purpose: SC3 / D-09 idempotency must actually hold for the new source type.
Output: award route writes deterministic `custom_block` source ids and reads
entries within the caller's family only. (The DB-side RLS hardening, backfill of
already-awarded rows, and the exploit-path verify probe are Plan 08, which depends
on the exact source-id format chosen here.)
</objective>
